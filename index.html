01 The Situation

The DDA's public mandate is growing faster than its staff count. Eight Follow the Sun companies in the most recent cohort, 684 committed jobs, three-year reporting windows on each. A City Hall review of the agency's budget and governance this spring. An expanding AI Digital Twin partnership with the City's Economic Innovation and Development team. All three of these are document-and-decision workflows sitting on top of an 11-to-50-person team that already runs enhanced services, Follow the Sun, Flagler Street, and quarterly board obligations.

02 The Frame

Most of the reporting work at a public authority is not an AI problem. It is a traditional-code problem dressed as an AI problem. Our working rule is 60/30/10: roughly 60 percent traditional software, 30 percent rule-based logic, 10 percent actual AI. We would start by asking which parts of the Follow the Sun compliance workflow and the board-packet cycle are genuinely AI-shaped (narrative summaries, unstructured document review) and which parts are structured data moves a database should handle. The goal is a workflow the DDA can explain in a public meeting.

03 The Case on Record

VigilOre is a multi-agent compliance platform built by Eduba for a mining-sector operator in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. Regulated reporting that used to take 160-plus staff hours per cycle now runs in under 5 minutes, with an auditable trail. The shape of that problem (multiple document types, threshold verification, external scrutiny) is the same shape as Follow the Sun grant compliance and board-packet production.

04 The Method

The method behind this work is the Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems. ICM organizes how an agent uses context as a layered filesystem, which gives a public agency a defensible, auditable way to explain any AI-assisted decision in a public meeting. That matters more for the DDA than for most buyers, because every contract and workflow is subject to Florida Sunshine and public-records review.
